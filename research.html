“Is Rich Phenomenology Fragmented?”

Published in Synthese, 2025. This paper challenges the assumption that the richness of experience entails unity. I propose a modular account of consciousness grounded in disunified perceptual modalities. [Read]

“Do Semantic Properties Involve the Future?”

This paper lies at the intersection of philosophy of language, epistemology, metaphysics, and even philosophy of mind. Very roughly, its core idea is to examine the connection between “revisions in belief and practice” and “changes in meaning,” and then to use that connection to argue against Temporal Externalism (a view that semantic reference can be determined by future facts). In the final part, I argue that TE can only be made plausible by presupposing a kind of meta-belief—which, however, undercuts the very motivation behind externalism.

“New Object Theory and Illusion”

Defends a novel account of perceptual objecthood that accommodates visual illusions without invoking representational error. The framework draws on relationalist views of perceptual content and metaphysical minimalism.
