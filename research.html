Work and Writings in Philosophy of Mind and Language

Publications

“Is Rich Phenomenology Fragmented?”
Synthese, 2025. [Springer]
Argues against the assumption that phenomenal richness entails phenomenal unity. Defends a modular view of perceptual experience, grounded in empirical disunity across modalities.

Work in Progress

“Do Semantic Properties Involve the Future?”
Investigates whether the truth-conditions of natural language expressions require reference to future events. Explores implications for semantic anti-realism and temporal externalism.
“New Object Theory and Illusion”
Defends a novel theory of perceptual objecthood that accommodates visual illusions without invoking representational error. Draws on relational views of perceptual content.
